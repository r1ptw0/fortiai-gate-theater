Securing AI Agents
We've spent years — and billions of dollars — securing enterprise data, applications, and infrastructure.

Now we're exposing that same data to large language models and autonomous agents. The question for today: how do we secure that interaction layer?
The Conversation We Came To Have
How do we secure
the interaction layer?
Thousands to millions spent on firewalls, NGFWs, SIEM, DLP, and zero trust — all to keep sensitive data inside the perimeter.
Now every AI prompt sends that data out. Source code, PII, security telemetry, credentials — flowing to third-party LLMs with every keystroke, every query, every automated workflow.
The AI proxy is the missing control point. Prompt inspection, DLP, output validation, rate limiting — one governance layer between your users and every AI model they call.
The OWASP Top 10 for LLMs is your framework. Map every risk — LLM01 prompt injection, LLM02 insecure output, LLM06 sensitive data — to the control that addresses it. The proxy is where most of them land.
How do we ensure the tool calls these agents make are trusted? How do we validate the data they retrieve? How do we protect against prompt injection, poisoned sources, and excessive permissions?

You wouldn't let network traffic flow without a firewall — the same discipline now has to extend to how agents reason, retrieve, and act.
